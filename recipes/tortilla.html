Tortilla

Description

A Spanish tortilla, also known as a potato omelette, is a traditional dish from Spain made with potatoes, onions, and eggs. It is typically cooked in a large, shallow pan and is often served as a tapa, or small snack, in bars and restaurants. Spanish tortillas are usually sliced into wedges and can be served hot or at room temperature. They are a popular choice for a quick and easy meal and are often served with a salad or other vegetables. They can be made with a variety of ingredients, such as bell peppers, chorizo, and other vegetables, but the basic recipe consists of potatoes, onions, and eggs.

Ingredients

  • 2 potatoes, peeled and sliced into thin rounds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a large, non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Add the potatoes and onion, and cook until the potatoes are tender and the onion is translucent.
  2. Beat the eggs in a bowl and season with salt. Add the eggs to the pan with the potatoes and onion, and stir gently to combine.
  3. Cook the tortilla until the edges start to set, then use a spatula to carefully lift the edges and allow the uncooked egg to flow underneath.
  4. When the tortilla is mostly set, carefully slide it onto a plate, then place the pan upside down over the plate and flip the pan and plate together to invert the tortilla back into the pan.
  5. Cook the other side of the tortilla until it is golden brown and fully set.
  6. Serve the tortilla warm, cut into wedges.
