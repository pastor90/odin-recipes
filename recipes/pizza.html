Pizza

Description

Margherita pizza is a classic pizza that originated in Naples, Italy. It is made with a simple tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil, and is traditionally baked in a wood-fired oven. The ingredients are arranged in layers on top of a thin, crispy crust, and the pizza is baked until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Margherita pizza is named after Queen Margherita of Savoy, who was said to have been served a pizza with the same ingredients by Raffaele Esposito, the owner of a pizzeria in Naples, in 1889. The red, white, and green colors of the ingredients were meant to mimic the Italian flag, and the pizza quickly became popular as a symbol of national pride. Today, margherita pizza is enjoyed all over the world and is considered one of the most classic and beloved types of pizza.

Ingredients

  • 237 mL warm water (43-46°C)
  • 11 g active dry yeast
  • 454 g all-purpose flour
  • 30 mL olive oil
  • 5 mL salt
  • 2 mL sugar
  • 250 g mozzarella cheese, sliced or shredded
  • fresh basil leaves
  • 8-10 cherry tomatoes, sliced

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the water and yeast. Let sit for a few minutes until the yeast is activated and foamy.
  2. Add the flour, olive oil, salt, and sugar to the bowl. Mix until the dough comes together in a ball.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover with a damp cloth. Set aside in a warm place to rise for about 1 hour, until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F).
  6. On a floured surface, roll out the dough into a large circle or rectangle, depending on your preference. Transfer the dough to a baking sheet or pizza pan.
  7. Spread the tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Top with the mozzarella cheese and sliced cherry tomatoes.
  8. Bake the pizza for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Let the pizza cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil, if desired. Enjoy!
